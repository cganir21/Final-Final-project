Top Three Most Inspiring Rock Climbs

1. Alex Honnold’s Ascent of El Capitan (2017)

In 2017, Alex Honnold made history by becoming the first person to free solo El Capitan in Yosemite National Park, completing the Freerider route in a mind-boggling 3 hours, 56 minutes. El Capitan, standing at 3,000 feet (914 meters), is one of the most iconic rock formations in the world, and free soloing it without ropes was an unprecedented feat in climbing. Honnold’s ascent required not only impeccable climbing technique but also extraordinary mental focus, as any mistake could have been fatal. His achievement was captured in the Oscar-winning documentary "Free Solo".


2. Chris Sharma’s Ascent of "Es Pontas" (2008)

In 2008, Chris Sharma became the first to free solo the legendary "Es Pontas" route, located on the island of Mallorca, Spain. This stunning climb is set on a natural arch, which requires not only immense climbing ability but also precision and a level of risk-taking that few climbers would attempt. "Es Pontas" is a 60-foot (18 meters) vertical rock face that leads to an arch above the ocean. Sharma’s ascent of this stunning and dangerous line is considered one of the defining moments of free solo climbing.

3. Adam Ondra’s Ascent of "Silence" (2017)

In 2017, Adam Ondra completed the world’s first 5.15d (9b+) route, "Silence", in the Flatanger Cave in Norway. While not a free solo, Ondra’s ascent of "Silence" is noteworthy because it is the hardest route ever climbed at that time. The climb took Ondra months of effort to complete, requiring not only peak physical ability but also an extraordinary mental game. It has since been regarded as one of the hardest sport climbing routes in the world. Ondra’s achievement pushed the boundaries of climbing difficulty and cemented his reputation as one of the greatest climbers in history.
